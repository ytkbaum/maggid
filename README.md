✨ What is Maggid?

Every great shiur is really a great story — an idea wrapped in a moment that listeners can feel, remember, and carry home. But preparing one is slow: hunting for the right mashal, the perfect quote, a maaseh that lands, then sequencing it all so the idea actually builds.

Maggid is an AI-powered platform that helps educators design and deliver shiurim that move people. You bring the idea; Maggid helps you find the stories, shape the arc, read the room, and ship polished materials — so you spend less time digging and more time teaching.



🚀 Features

🔍 Agentic source discovery

Describe your topic, sugya, or theme and Maggid's research agents go to work — surfacing relevant stories, quotes, anecdotes, and sources with context on where each comes from and why it fits. Less rummaging through your shelf and bookmarks; more curated raw material, on demand.

📡 Always current

Torah is timeless; the world your audience lives in is not. Maggid continuously scans current events, trending ideas, and fresh perspectives — from new books and TED talks to op-eds and the conversations people are actually having — and auto-updates with prompts that tie your shiur to the moment. Your material refreshes as the world does, so a talk never feels like it was written for last year's headlines.

🎬 Story-driven roadmaps

Structure isn't decoration — it's what makes an idea land. Maggid drafts class and speech roadmaps built on narrative architecture: a hook, rising tension, the turn, and a payoff that delivers your message with impact. Think the craft behind a great film, in service of the Torah you're teaching.

🎠 The curation carousel

As you build, swipe through curated stories, prompts, and sources ranked as top recommendations for this shiur. Keep what fits, dismiss what doesn't, and watch the suggestions sharpen as your shiur takes shape.

🎯 Audience- & circumstance-aware curation

The same idea needs a different telling depending not just on who is listening, but on where, when, and why. A candlelit cave in Tzfat and the prisoners' barracks at Birkenau ask for entirely different stories, pacing, and tone. Feed in your audience and your circumstances — level, age, and background, but also setting, mood, occasion, and the weight of the moment — and recommendations re-tune to fit the room you're actually standing in.

🧱 Whole-curriculum builder

Zoom out from a single shiur to an entire arc. Maggid helps you design complete curricula that are comprehensive (nothing essential left out), cohesive (each session builds on the last), and optimally sequenced and timed so ideas land in the right order for real understanding. It plans around teachable moments and weaves in everything that happens between classes — readings, reflections, activities, and assignments — so the learning continues long after the shiur ends and the ideas truly stick.

📚 Learn from the masters

Duplicate and remix shiurim from top educators. Start from a proven framework, study how a master built tension and arrived at the point, then make it your own — a living library of pedagogy you can stand on.

👤 Profiles, sharing & collaboration

Build your educator profile, publish your best work, and collaborate with colleagues. Share drafts, gather feedback, and refine together — a community of educators raising each other's craft.

📣 Publish & repurpose

One shiur, many outputs. Spin off quick segments, clips, and marketing materials to promote a class or share a short idea — ready to hand to other educators or post for your community.

🖼️ Gorgeous handouts & slides

Maggid produces beautiful, presentation-ready handouts and slides automatically — source sheets, talking points, and visuals — so your materials look as good as your shiur sounds.



🛠 How it works

  1. Set the idea        → topic, sugya, or theme + the takeaway you want
  2. Describe the room   → audience level, setting, size, vibe
  3. Discover            → agents surface stories, quotes, sources
  4. Shape the arc       → drag the carousel picks into a narrative roadmap
  5. Refine              → tune pacing, swap sources, invite feedback
  6. Publish             → export handouts, slides, clips & promo
