## 🌟 Vision

Every generation of Jewish leadership has carried ideas — initiatives, institutions, programs, communities — from a spark into reality. The process has always demanded the same things: clarity of vision, the right people, disciplined execution, and relentless follow-through.

**Maggid** is built to accelerate that process.

Named for the voice that gives form to thought, Maggid is a platform where Jewish leaders and builders can:

- **Develop an idea from first principles** — working with an AI agent to sharpen concept, identify gaps, and stress-test assumptions
- **Identify and recruit the right people** — map out needed roles, post them, and build a team around the initiative
- **Manage development end-to-end** — from kernel to pilot to full execution, with structured milestones and accountability
- **Stay actively in command** — monitor what the team produces, give directed feedback, and keep the initiative on track
- **Streamline communication** — so the leader spends time leading, not managing logistics

This is not a project management tool with a Jewish skin. It is built from the ground up around how Jewish initiatives actually develop — with the nuances of communal leadership, Torah values, and the particular texture of Jewish organizational life.

## ⚙️ How It Works

```
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                     MAGGID PLATFORM                             │
│                                                               │
│  1. KERNEL          2. DEVELOPMENT       3. TEAM              │
│  ────────           ─────────────      ──────────────  │
│  Raw idea      →    Agent helps      →   Roles mapped,       │
│  or vision          shape, stress-       team recruited,      │
│                     test & define        contributors onboard │
│                                                               │
│  4. EXECUTION       5. MONITORING        6. DELIVERY          │
│  ─────────────      ─────────────      ────────────  │
│  Structured    →    Leader reviews  →   Initiative lands     │
│  milestones &        output, gives        fully executed      │
│  task flow          directed prompts                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Stage 1 — The Kernel
You arrive with an idea — a program, an institution, a community initiative, a shiur series, a social enterprise. It doesn't need to be fully formed. Maggid starts where you are.

### Stage 2 — Agent-Assisted Development
The Maggid agent works with you to bring the concept into focus: clarifying purpose, identifying assumptions, stress-testing viability, and producing a structured concept document. You lead the thinking; the agent accelerates it.

### Stage 3 — Role Mapping & Team Building
Once the concept is clear, Maggid helps map the human infrastructure: what roles are needed, what skills they require, and how to attract the right people. Post roles, review candidates, and build your team inside the platform.

### Stage 4 — Structured Execution
Milestones, tasks, and owners — broken down to the level where things actually happen. The agent generates first-draft work plans; the leader adjusts and approves.

### Stage 5 — Active Monitoring
The leader stays in command without being in the weeds. Review what's produced, give directed feedback, track progress against milestones, and surface blockers early.

### Stage 6 — Delivery
The initiative lands. Retrospectives, documentation, and handoff protocols ensure the knowledge lives beyond the launch.

## 🧠 The Maggid Agent

The agent is the heartbeat of the platform. It is not a chatbot bolted onto a task manager — it is a genuine thinking partner for the leader, purpose-built for the development of initiatives.

### What the agent does

**In concept development:**
- Asks the right clarifying questions to surface what you actually mean
- Identifies what is assumed vs. what is known
- Maps the landscape of similar initiatives and what can be learned from them
- Produces structured concept documents ready for team or funder review

**In team building:**
- Drafts role descriptions from a brief
- Suggests what types of people fit each role
- Helps evaluate fit during recruitment

**In execution:**
- Converts milestones into actionable task breakdowns
- Drafts prompts and briefs for team members
- Surfaces blockers and proposes solutions

**In monitoring:**
- Summarizes what was produced and flags gaps
- Compares output to the original concept and milestones
- Suggests directed feedback from leader to team

> The leader always drives. The agent always serves.

## 👥 Building Your Team

Maggid recognizes that the right team is as important as the right idea. The platform is built to help leaders think clearly about human infrastructure — not just org charts, but the actual competencies an initiative needs at each stage.

### Typical role categories

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| 🎯 **Vision & Strategy** | Co-founder, strategic advisor, domain expert |
| 📃 **Content & Curriculum** | Writer, curriculum developer, subject-matter expert |
| 💻 **Technology** | Developer, designer, data architect |
| 📣 **Communications** | Community manager, copywriter, social media |
| 🤝 **Operations** | Project manager, coordinator, logistics |
| 💰 **Development** | Fundraiser, grants writer, partnership manager |
| 📚 **Torah & Halachah** | Rabbinic advisor, posek, dayan |

Roles are not static — they evolve as the initiative develops. Maggid tracks what is needed now, what is needed next, and what can wait.
